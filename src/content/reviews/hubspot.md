## Core Features

HubSpot offers a unified suite of tools across CRM, Marketing Hub, Sales Hub, Service Hub, and Content Hub. The free CRM includes contact management, deal tracking, and email integration. Paid tiers unlock advanced automation, custom reporting, and AI-powered features like Breeze AI for predictive lead scoring and content generation. Key features include:

- **Marketing Hub**: Email marketing, landing pages, SEO, social media scheduling, and analytics.
- **Sales Hub**: Pipeline management, meeting scheduling, quotes, and conversation intelligence.
- **Service Hub**: Ticketing, knowledge base, live chat, and customer feedback surveys.
- **Content Hub**: CMS, blogging, and video hosting.
- **Operations Hub**: Data sync, programmable automation, and custom objects.

## Pricing Breakdown

HubSpot's pricing is modular and scales with features. As of 2026:

- **Free CRM**: $0 – unlimited users, basic contact management, and limited marketing emails.
- **Starter**: $20–$50/month per hub – adds automation, forms, and basic reporting.
- **Professional**: $500–$1,800/month per hub – full automation, custom reporting, and team management.
- **Enterprise**: $1,200–$4,500+/month per hub – advanced permissions, multi-touch attribution, and custom objects.

Most businesses need multiple hubs, so costs add up quickly. For example, Marketing Hub Professional + Sales Hub Professional starts around $2,300/month.

## Ease of Use

The interface is clean and intuitive for basic tasks, but the sheer number of features can be overwhelming. Setup requires time to configure workflows, properties, and integrations. HubSpot Academy offers free training, but advanced customization often needs developer support. Mobile apps are functional but limited.

## Customer Support

Support varies by plan: free users get community and knowledge base; paid plans include email and live chat; Enterprise gets phone support and a dedicated account manager. Response times are generally good, but complex issues may require escalation.

## Integrations

HubSpot integrates with over 1,000 apps via its App Marketplace, including Salesforce, Slack, Shopify, and Google Workspace. The Operations Hub provides bi-directional sync for clean data. Custom API access is available on higher tiers.

## Security & Compliance

SOC 2 Type II certified, GDPR compliant, and offers data encryption at rest and in transit. Enterprise plans include advanced permissions and audit logs.

## Final Verdict

HubSpot is a robust platform for businesses that can afford its premium pricing and have the bandwidth to learn its ecosystem. It's less suitable for very small teams or those needing a simple, low-cost CRM. The free plan is a great starting point, but scaling costs should be carefully evaluated.
