## Core Features

Notion combines note-taking, project management, databases, and wikis into one platform. Key features include:
- **Databases**: Relational databases with views (table, board, calendar, gallery, list).
- **AI Agents**: Built-in AI for writing, summarizing, and generating content.
- **Templates**: Hundreds of pre-built templates for various use cases.
- **Collaboration**: Real-time editing, comments, and permissions.
- **Integrations**: Connects with Slack, Google Drive, GitHub, and more.

## Pricing Breakdown

Notion offers a free tier with limited blocks and file uploads. Paid plans:
- **Plus**: $10/user/month (unlimited blocks, file uploads up to 5MB).
- **Business**: $18/user/month (advanced permissions, team spaces).
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing (SAML SSO, advanced security).

AI features are add-ons at $10/user/month.

## Ease of Use

Notion has a steep learning curve due to its flexibility. New users may find the interface overwhelming, but once accustomed, it becomes highly efficient. The mobile app is less polished than the desktop version.

## Performance & Reliability

Notion is generally reliable, but offline mode is limited to cached pages. Large databases can slow down, and the mobile app may lag.

## Customer Support

Support is primarily via email and knowledge base. Response times vary; no phone support. Enterprise plans get priority.

## Alternatives

- **Coda**: Similar flexibility with better formulas.
- **Confluence**: Better for enterprise wikis.
- **ClickUp**: More robust project management features.

## Verdict

Notion is a top choice for teams that need a customizable, all-in-one workspace. However, its complexity and performance issues make it less suitable for large enterprises or users who prefer simplicity.
